This note contains information about different licenses that are commonly used on Modrinth. Mainly I have this note here as quick reference to check which mods require additional attribution on the mod packs' home pages.

## Lucent

[[index|Lucent Adventures]] itself is licensed under an `LGPL-3.0-or-later`.

The original content in the documentation for Lucent is licensed under `CC BY-SA 4.0`. Read the full license [here](https://github.com/lucent-mc/docs/blob/v4/content/LICENSE.md). For anyone wanting to use, adapt or redistribute this documentation, this means they are required to give credit to this wiki as its source, and license any adaptations of our content under the same license terms.

The externally sourced content in the documentation for Lucent necessarily complies with the original source's license terms. If the source has a permissive, copyleft license, that doesn't require our redistribution to be licensed under the same terms, it will still be licensed under the source license. It does not make sense for us to impose license terms on content that we did not write.

Icons in the titles are from [Streamline Vector](https://www.streamlinehq.com/icons/pixel-icons). Licensed under `CC BY 4.0`. Hence this attribution notice.

## ARR

ARR is short for All Rights Reserved. This means that the code cannot be adapted, copies or redistributed without the author's explicit permission. All mods using ARR will have additional attribution on Lucent packs. This is not _required_ as per ARR specifications, but we do this in good faith to cover edge cases.

## CC

A **Creative Commons (CC) license** is one of several [public copyright licenses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_copyright_license 'Public copyright license') that enable the free distribution of an otherwise [copyrighted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright 'Copyright') "work". A CC license is used when an author wants to give other people the right to share, use, and build upon a work that the author has created.

All CC licenses can be found [here](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/).

CC licenses (except for `CC0`) require attribution.

`BY` - Credit must be given to the creator (Attribution).  
`NC` - Only **noncommercial** use of the work is permitted.  
`ND` - **No derivate** works or adaptations are permitted.  
`SA` - Derivative works or adaptations must be **Shared Alike** (under the same license terms).

License versions:

- `CC BY 4.0`
- `CC BY-SA 4.0`
- `CC BY-NC 4.0`
- `CC BY-NC-SA 4.0`
- `CC BY-ND 4.0`
- `CC BY-NC-ND 4.0`
- `CC0 1.0` - Copyright on the work has been waived, and the work has been dedicated to the public domain.

## GPL

GPL or GNU General Public License, are a series of widely used [free software licenses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_software_license), or [copyleft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyleft) licenses, that guarantee [end users](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/End_user) the freedom to run, study, share, or modify the software...[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License)

Most notably, GPL is copyleft, which means that any derivative work must be distributed under the same or equivalent license terms.

GPL does not require attribution.

License versions:

- `GPL-3.0-or-later`
- `GPL-3.0-only`
- `GPL-2.0-or-later`
- `GPL-2.0-only`
- `GPL-1.0-or-later`
- `GPL-1.0-only`

## AGPL

The GNU Affero General Public License (GNU AGPL) is a [free](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_software_movement) [copyleft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyleft) license published by the [Free Software Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Software_Foundation) in November 2007, based on the [GNU GPL version 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License#Version_3) and the Affero General Public License (non-GNU). It is intended for software designed to be run over a [network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_network), adding a provision requiring that the corresponding [source code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Source_code) of modified versions of the software be prominently offered to all users who interact with the software over a network...  
[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_Affero_General_Public_License)

Similar to GPL, AGPL does not require attribution.

License versions:

- `AGPL-3.0-or-later`
- `AGPL-3.0-only`

## LGPL

The **GNU Lesser General Public License** (**LGPL**) is a [free-software license](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free-software_license 'Free-software license') published by the [Free Software Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Software_Foundation 'Free Software Foundation') (FSF). The license allows developers and companies to use and integrate a software component released under the LGPL into their own (even [proprietary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proprietary_software 'Proprietary software')) software without being required by the terms of a strong [copyleft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyleft 'Copyleft') license to release the source code of their own components. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_Lesser_General_Public_License)

In short, this is GPL but unlike GPL you don't have to distribute your entire derivative codebase under the same license terms. You only have to distribute your adaptations of the original source under LGPL or equivalent.

Like GPL and AGPL, LGPL does not require attribution

License versions:

- `LGPL-3.0-or-later`
- `LGPL-3.0-only`
- `LGPL-2.1-or-later`
- `LGPL-2.1-only`
- `LGPL-2.0-or-later`
- `LGPL-2.0-only`

## Apache

The **Apache License** is a [permissive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permissive_free_software_license 'Permissive free software license') free software license written by the [Apache Software Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apache_Software_Foundation 'Apache Software Foundation') (ASF). It allows users to use the software for any purpose, to distribute it, to modify it, and to distribute modified versions of the software under the terms of the license, without concern for [royalties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royalties 'Royalties'). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apache_License)

Apache requires attribution. From `Apache-1.1` on it doesn't require attribution in advertising, only in documentation.

License versions:

- `Apache-2.0`
- `Apache-1.1`
- `Apache-1.0`

## Polyform

[PolyForm](https://polyformproject.org/what-is-polyform/) is a project to draft and make freely available plain-language source code licenses with limited rights.

Polyform licenses require attribution in the form of a copyright notice with a link to the source.

License versions:

- `PolyForm-Shield`
- `PolyForm-Strict`
- `PolyForm-Noncommercial`
- `PolyForm-Free-Trial`
- `PolyForm-Internal-Use`
- `PolyForm-Small-Business`
- `PolyForm-Perimeter`

## MPL

The **Mozilla Public License** (**MPL**) is a [free and open-source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_and_open-source_software 'Free and open-source software') [weak copyleft license](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_copyleft 'Weak copyleft') for most [Mozilla Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozilla_Foundation 'Mozilla Foundation') [software](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software 'Software') such as [Firefox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firefox 'Firefox') and [Thunderbird](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozilla_Thunderbird 'Mozilla Thunderbird'). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozilla_Public_License)

MPL does not require attribution.

License versions:

- `MPL-2.0`
- `MPL-1.1`
- `MPL-1.0`

## MIT

The **MIT License** is a [permissive software license](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permissive_software_license 'Permissive software license') originating at the [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology 'Massachusetts Institute of Technology') (MIT)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIT_License#cite_note-6) in the late 1980s.[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIT_License#cite_note-history-7) As a permissive license, it puts few restrictions on reuse and has high [license compatibility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/License_compatibility 'License compatibility'). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIT_License)

MIT does not require attribution.

## Unlicense

The **Unlicense** is a [public domain equivalent license](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_domain_equivalent_license 'Public domain equivalent license') for software which provides a [public domain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_domain 'Public domain') [waiver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waiver 'Waiver') with a fall-back public-domain-like license, similar to the [CC Zero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CC_Zero 'CC Zero') for cultural works. It includes language used in earlier software projects and has a focus on an [anti-copyright](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-copyright 'Anti-copyright') message. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unlicense)

## Other

Some mod packs provide different or unique licenses which we will indicate with "Other" in the frontmatter / metadata. The licenses will still be linked to, and any required additional information will be included on each respective mod page.
